  1. inclusive
    Towards more inclusive linguistics research and application
    Cong Zhang
    In Language and Linguistics Guest Speaker Seminar. Newcastle University 2023
    Abs Bib

    This talk will address the issue of imbalanced development in linguistics between well-resourced and under-resourced languages. One of the primary reasons for this imbalance is the lack of research tools and resources for under-resourced languages, including for data collection, analysis, and processing. This can make it challenging for linguists to conduct research on under-resourced languages, as they may not have access to the same level of technology and infrastructure that is available for well-resourced languages. Addressing this issue is an essential step towards promoting inclusivity in the field of linguistics. In this talk, I will present some research findings, tools, and resources that have been developed to facilitate linguistic research on under-resourced languages, including options for remote phonetic data collection, an automatic phonetic recogniser and aligner which can be trained with data from different language varieties, and a grapheme-to-phoneme modelling framework and relevant resources, which can help researchers work with under-resourced languages.

  2. Edinburgh
    Kaleidoscopic boundary tones
    Cong Zhang
    In Linguistics Circle Series. University of Edinburgh 2022
    Abs Bib HTML

    Both lexical tone and intonation are mainly realised through pitch modulation. This often causes the misunderstanding of tonal languages having no intonation or limited intonation. In fact, tonal languages can have more complex prosodic patterns than expected. In this talk, I will present how different types of boundary tones – floating boundary tone and actual boundary tones (mono-tonal, bi-tonal, and more) – can be used for a range of linguistic and para-linguistic functions in Tianjin Mandarin and Standard Mandarin.

  4. AI
    Applying Linguistics Knowledge to Speech Technology
    Cong Zhang
    In AAII Technical Lectures Series. Assam Artificial Intelligence Initiative, India 2022
    Abs Bib HTML Presentation

    Speech technology has been advancing at an unprecedented speed due to the revitalisation of machine learning and ever-so-growing available data. Whilst speech technology deals with language, linguistic knowledge is used less and less in the process. However, using what we already know about languages may give us an advantage over the black-box algorithms. Making use of up-to-date and suitable linguistics knowledge can reduce the amount of data needed, save time and costs, allow the models to be more interpretable and controllable, and eventually may lead to new methodological development. In this talk, I will talk about how linguistics knowledge can be applied to ASR and TTS in various ways, in hope of bridging the gap between linguistics and engineering.

  1. Korean
    Perceiving and modelling the scope of question focus in Korean
    Stephen Jones, Yoolim Kim, and Cong Zhang
    In Speech Prosody and Beyond Workshop 2023
    Abs Bib HTML PDF

    This paper aims to more precisely specify the nature of pitch expansion in question focus in Korean, as a precursor to the computational modelling of a discriminative learning process based on prosodic cues. The work is important because it strengthens a potential account that allows formal links between prosody and syntactic analysis. In focusing on phenomena at the trailing edge of the scope of focus, it can be used alongside left-to-right statistical machine translation to improve the output of speech recognition systems, without requiring a full statistical parse of the input, and in speech generation systems to reduce the ambiguity of output.
